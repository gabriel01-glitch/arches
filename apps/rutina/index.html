ARCHES · Daily Warm-Up

Morning Articulation Routine

72 seconds. Every morning, before you speak English.
Streak: 0 days
Repeat each word right after you hear it.
1

FINAL SOUNDS

Finish every ending. Make the final mouth position clearly, and never add a vowel. (No te comas el final.)
help · helps · helped
ask · asks · asked · just · fast
Why: Spanish often softens the ends of syllables, and Caribbean Spanish most of all. In English those endings carry grammar: helped sounds like help + t, asked like ask + t. Never "help-ed".
2

VOWEL PAIRS

sheep, beat: tongue high and forward. ship, bit: tongue a little lower and more central, not just shorter. cat, bad: jaw open, tongue forward. cut, bud: small opening, tongue central.
sheep vs ship · beat vs bit
cat vs cut · bad vs bud
Why: Spanish has 5 vowels, English has many more. These pairs change the word completely.
3

TH

Tongue lightly between your teeth, and keep the air flowing. Voice off: thin, think. Voice on: they, this. (La lengua sale, no se esconde.)
tin vs thin · day vs they · think · this
Why: without continuous air, TH turns into T or D. If the air keeps moving over your tongue, you are doing it right.
4

LINKING

Keep the ending, then connect immediately. No pause, no extra vowel.
stop → stop it
asked → asked them
turn it on (like one word)
Why: protect the final sound, then glue the words together: stop it sounds like "stopit". This is how native speakers glide over small words.
5

AMERICAN R

Curl or pull the tongue back. The tip touches nothing. No tap, no trill.
rrr · red · right · road
Why: American r allows different tongue shapes and they all work. The one rule: never tap like Spanish r. Hold "rrr" for 3 seconds before the words.
6

REAL SPEECH

Say it slowly and clearly. Then say it naturally, keeping the clear endings. Punch the red words, glide over the rest. Pick any sentence below.
"I just asked them to turn it on first."
Why: one sentence with everything: final sounds and clusters, linking, th, and rhythm. This is the bridge to real conversation.
Routine complete. Your mouth is awake. 🎉

Tips

  • Stand or sit up straight, and exaggerate every movement.
  • Do this every morning before speaking or studying English.
  • Once a week, record step 6 and send it to your teacher. Pick one correction to focus on next week.
  • Consistency builds strong pronunciation habits.
